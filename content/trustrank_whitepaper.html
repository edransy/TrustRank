§0 What this document is

The pitch and whitepaper establish a per-claim convergence engine: N LLM scorers submit belief vectors, an on-chain operator takes a stake-×-reputation weighted average, outliers are slashed, and dimensions settle under an ε-N rule.

This document supplies the missing global layer. Reputation in OracleCore.sol is currently a local scalar — a running product of one agent's own past alignment. That is link-count thinking. PageRank's central lesson is that authority is recursive and global.

TrustRank = PageRank's recursive eigenvector authority + TrustRank/Anti-TrustRank spam-defense papers (Gyöngyi, Garcia-Molina, Pedersen 2004; Krishnan & Raj 2006) + crypto-native capital-at-risk.

§1 The lineage, made exact

PageRank's web vector $r$ is the stationary distribution of a random surfer:

PageRank
$$r \;=\; d\,P\,r \;+\; (1-d)\,\frac{1}{N}\mathbf{1}, \qquad P_{ji}=\frac{A_{ij}}{\sum_k A_{ik}}$$

with $A$ the binary link matrix, $d\approx 0.85$ the damping factor, and a uniform teleport $\tfrac{1}{N}\mathbf{1}$.

The 2004 TrustRank spam paper changed exactly one thing: replace the uniform teleport with a biased seed vector $s^{+}$ supported on a hand-vetted set of known-good pages. Authority is now conservation-law constrained: the only way trust enters the system is through the seed.

TrustRank-for-crypto changes three things relative to web TrustRank:

  1. Edges are bought. A web hyperlink is free; a TrustRank edge is a staked bond $B_{ij}$. Topology now has a price.
  2. Seeds are oracular, not manual. The seed set is produced automatically by the anchor engine — events that cannot be faked by a cartel.
  3. Distrust is enforced, not just propagated. When distrust flows back along an edge that contradicted an anchor, the edge's bond burns (slashing).

§2 The trust graph G

2.1 Nodes and edges

Let $\mathcal{N}$ be the set of entities, $|\mathcal{N}| = N$. A directed edge $i \to j$ means "$i$ has staked a bonded vouch/review on $j$." Each edge carries three time-stamped quantities at epoch $t$:

SymbolMeaningRange
$B_{ij} \ge 0$Live bond $i$ has at risk on its review of $j$ (wei)$[0,\infty)$
$q_{ij}\in[0,1]$Evidence quality of the review (citations, source diversity, co-reviewer agreement)$[0,1]$
$f_{ij}(t)=e^{-(t-t_{ij})/H}$Freshness decay, half-life governed by $H$$(0,1]$

The edge weight is the product:

Edge weight
$$a_{ij} \;=\; B_{ij}\cdot q_{ij}\cdot f_{ij}(t)$$

Define out-weight and in-weight:

$$a_i^{\text{out}}=\sum_{j} a_{ij}, \qquad a_j^{\text{in}}=\sum_{i} a_{ij}$$

2.2 Transition operator

The forward (trust-flow) transition is row-stochastic over out-edges:

Transition matrix
$$M_{ij} \;=\;\begin{cases}\dfrac{a_{ij}}{a_i^{\text{out}}}, & a_i^{\text{out}}>0,\\[1.2em] 0, & a_i^{\text{out}}=0 \;\;(\text{dangling node}).\end{cases}$$

Let $P = M^{\top}$ (column-stochastic on non-dangling columns); $P_{ji}$ is the probability a trust-walker at $i$ steps to $j$. Dangling nodes are handled exactly as in PageRank: their mass is redirected to the seed vector via the dangling indicator $u\in\{0,1\}^N$, $u_i=\mathbb{1}[a_i^{\text{out}}=0]$.

§3 The TrustRank operator (forward authority)

3.1 Definition

Let $s^{+}\in\Delta^{N-1}$ be the normalized good-seed teleport vector (§5), $\mathbf{1}^\top s^{+}=1$, $s^{+}>0$. The TrustRank vector $t\in\mathbb{R}^N$ is the fixed point:

Core equation (TR)
$$t \;=\; d\,\big(P + s^{+}u^{\top}\big)\,t \;+\; (1-d)\,s^{+} \tag{TR}$$

Component form — node $j$ is trusted to the degree that trusted nodes spend their out-weight on it, plus its own seed grounding:

$$t_j \;=\; d\!\!\sum_{i\,:\,i\to j}\frac{a_{ij}}{a_i^{\text{out}}}\,t_i \;+\; d\,s_j^{+}\!\!\sum_{i\,\text{dangling}}\!\! t_i \;+\;(1-d)\,s_j^{+}$$

3.2 Existence, uniqueness, convergence

Define the Google matrix:

Google matrix
$$G \;=\; d\big(P + s^{+}u^{\top}\big) + (1-d)\,s^{+}\mathbf{1}^{\top}$$
Theorem 3.1 — Well-posedness

By Perron–Frobenius, $G$ has a unique stationary vector $t = Gt$ with eigenvalue $1$, $t>0$, $\mathbf{1}^\top t = 1$. The power iteration $t^{(k+1)}=Gt^{(k)}$ converges to $t$ from any stochastic start, geometrically:

$$\big\lVert t^{(k)} - t \big\rVert_1 \;\le\; C\, d^{\,k}$$

Proof. The subdominant eigenvalue of a personalized Google matrix obeys $|\lambda_2|\le d$ (Haveliwala–Kamvar 2003); the rest is the standard PageRank argument. □

3.3 The damping factor is the anti-collusion invariant

The pitch's single critical invariant is the reward split $\text{reward}_i=\beta\cdot\text{agr}_{\text{anchors}}+(1-\beta)\cdot\text{agr}_{\text{peers}}$, with $\beta\ge0.6$. In TrustRank this is literally the teleport mass:

Anti-collusion invariant (INV)
$$1-d \;=\; \beta \;\ge\; 0.6 \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad d \;\le\; 0.4 \tag{INV}$$
Why crypto-TrustRank uses $d\le 0.4$, not web PageRank's $0.85$. Google can trust topology ($d=0.85$) because faking millions of links is expensive at web scale. In adversarial DeFi, links are cheap to manufacture, so we cap the topology channel at $d\le0.4$ and force $\ge0.6$ of all authority to originate from capital-grounded anchors. (INV) is exactly the pitch invariant, re-derived as a spectral parameter.

§4 Anti-TrustRank (backward distrust) and net trust

4.1 Distrust propagation

Trust flows forward from good seeds; distrust flows backward from bad seeds to whoever vouched for them. Reverse transition over in-edges: $R_{ji}=a_{ij}/a_j^{\text{in}}$. The Anti-TrustRank vector $\delta\in\mathbb{R}^N$:

Anti-TrustRank (ATR)
$$\delta \;=\; d\,\big(R^{\top}+ s^{-}u_{\text{in}}^{\top}\big)\,\delta \;+\;(1-d)\,s^{-} \tag{ATR}$$

4.2 Net trust score

Let $\hat{t}_i = t_i/\max_k t_k$ and $\hat{\delta}_i=\delta_i/\max_k\delta_k$ be max-normalized to $[0,1]$. Two interchangeable forms:

Net score — two forms
$$\tau_i \;=\; \hat{t}_i\,\big(1-\hat{\delta}_i\big) \qquad\text{(multiplicative gate)}$$ $$\tau_i \;=\; \big[\hat{t}_i - \lambda\,\hat{\delta}_i\big]_+ \quad\text{(linear penalty, } \lambda\ge1)$$

The multiplicative gate is the default: $\hat{\delta}_i = 1$ drives $\tau_i$ to zero regardless of how high $\hat{t}_i$ is. An entity cannot have a good TrustRank score if its vouches have historically led the trust walker toward provably-bad outcomes.

4.3 Spam-mass: the topology-vs-grounding diagnostic

Compute a topology-only centrality $c$ (TrustRank with uniform teleport) alongside the grounded $t$ (anchor teleport). Define:

SpamMass
$$\text{SpamMass}_i \;=\; \frac{\big[c_i - t_i\big]_+}{c_i}\in[0,1]$$

$\text{SpamMass}_i \to 1$ is the signature of a mutual-vouch cartel: a cluster of well-connected nodes that point at each other but have no connection to any anchor-grounded entity. This is an early-warning system — it flags manipulation before a real-world failure event.

§5 Seeds from anchors (the oracle that can't be bought)

Let $\mathcal{E}_t$ be the anchor events observed up to epoch $t$, each with sign $\sigma_e\in\{+,-\}$, target entity $j(e)$, confidence $c_e\in[0,1]$, and recency weight $\phi_e=e^{-(t-t_e)/H_{\!A}}$.

Raw seeds
$$\tilde{s}^{+}_j=\sum_{e:\,\sigma_e=+,\,j(e)=j} c_e\,\phi_e, \qquad \tilde{s}^{-}_j=\sum_{e:\,\sigma_e=-,\,j(e)=j} c_e\,\phi_e$$

Smooth with a uniform floor $\varepsilon_s>0$ and normalize:

Normalized seeds
$$s^{+} = (1-\varepsilon_s)\frac{\tilde{s}^{+}}{\mathbf{1}^\top\tilde{s}^{+}} + \varepsilon_s\frac{1}{N}\mathbf{1}, \qquad s^{-} = \frac{\tilde{s}^{-}}{\mathbf{1}^\top\tilde{s}^{-}}$$

The floor $\varepsilon_s\tfrac{1}{N}\mathbf{1}$ is a mathematical necessity — Theorem 3.1 requires $s^{+}>0$ (strictly positive everywhere) to guarantee irreducibility of the Google matrix. No cartel can mint a positive anchor or fake a negative one — the former requires real elapsed time and external institutional verification.

§6 Slashing as enforced distrust attenuation

When a negative anchor fires on $j$, the distrust flux crossing edge $i\to j$ in one walker step:

Distrust flux
$$\Phi_{ij} \;=\; d\,R_{ji}\,\delta_j \;=\; d\,\frac{a_{ij}}{a_j^{\text{in}}}\,\delta_j$$

The bond $B_{ij}$ that $i$ staked on its (now falsified) vouch of $j$ is slashed:

Slash formula
$$\text{slash}_{ij} \;=\; \min\!\Big(B_{ij},\;\; \kappa\,B_{ij}\,\hat{\Phi}_{ij}\,\big(1+\mu\,\text{SpamMass}_i\big)\Big)$$

where $\hat{\Phi}_{ij}=\Phi_{ij}/\max\Phi$ normalizes flux to $[0,1]$, $\kappa\in(0,1]$ is the base slash rate, and $\mu\ge0$ is the spam-mass amplifier. Slashed wei are burned to 0x…dEaD, not redistributed.

This is the graph-level generalization of OracleCore.sol's current per-spec deviation slash: a global, multi-hop test rather than a local, single-round test.

§7 Coupling to the Temporal Trust Vector

7.1 Topic-sensitive reliability (5 reliabilities, not 1)

Run TrustRank per dimension $k\in\{$sec, liq, team, exec, harm$\}$ with dimension-specific seeds. Output: a reliability matrix:

$$r_i^{(k)} \;=\; \tau_i^{(k)} \;\in[0,1],\qquad k=1,\dots,5$$

7.2 The unified epoch update

Replace the scalar weight with a dimension-indexed TrustRank reliability:

Dimension-aware consensus
$$w_i^{(k)} \;=\; \text{stake}_i \cdot r_i^{(k)}, \qquad C^{(k)}_t \;=\; \frac{\sum_i w_i^{(k)}\,v_i^{(k)}}{\sum_i w_i^{(k)}}$$

Apply temporal smoothing (unchanged from pitch):

Temporal smoothing
$$V^{(k)}_{t+1} \;=\; (1-\alpha)\,V^{(k)}_t \;+\; \alpha\,C^{(k)}_t, \qquad \alpha=\sigma\!\big(w_1\text{conf}+w_2\text{age}+w_3\text{anchorFire}+w_4\text{varSpike}\big)$$

Reviewer reliabilities also smooth forward:

$$r^{(k)}_{i,t+1} = (1-\alpha)\,r^{(k)}_{i,t} + \alpha\,\tau^{(k)}_{i,t}$$

Full per-epoch pipeline: anchors → seeds → (TR)/(ATR) → $\tau^{(k)}$ → weights $w^{(k)}$ → consensus $C^{(k)}$ → smoothed $V^{(k)}$, with slashing applied on any negative-anchor fire.

§8 Sybil & collusion resistance

Theorem 8.1 — Closed cartels earn nothing

Let $C\subset\mathcal{N}$ be a clique with no good-seed mass ($s^{+}_i=0\ \forall i\in C$) and no incoming edges from $\mathcal{N}\setminus C$. Then:

$$\sum_{i\in C} t_i \;=\; (1-d)\sum_{i\in C}s^{+}_i \;=\; 0 \quad(\text{up to the }\varepsilon_s\text{ floor})$$

Proof. The sub-chain restricted to $C$ is isolated — it has no external inflow — so its stationary mass equals only the teleport contribution, which is zero. □

Theorem 8.2 — Bounded leakage

If outside parties direct total trust-flow $\theta$ into $C$, then:

$$\sum_{i\in C}t_i \le \dfrac{d}{1-d}\,\theta \le \dfrac{0.4}{0.6}\,\theta = \tfrac{2}{3}\theta \quad\text{under (INV)}$$
Theorem 8.3 — Attack-cost lower bound

To raise a fraudulent entity $X$ to score $\tau_X\ge\tau^*$, an adversary must source genuine trust-flow $\theta \ge \tfrac{1-d}{d}\,\tau^*\,\max_k t_k$ from non-Sybil vouchers. Profitable attack therefore requires:

$$\text{gain}(X) > \tfrac{1-d}{d}\,\tau^*\,\kappa\,\bar{B}$$

which the operator can set above any realizable extraction by choosing $\kappa$ and the minimum bond size $\bar{B}$.

Spectral + economic, not either alone. The (INV) constraint $d\le0.4$ caps topology amplification. Bonds make acquiring genuine trust-flow expensive. Slashing makes bond loss certain upon anchor fire. Spam-mass detects manipulation before anchors fire. None of the three mechanisms works alone — the combination is what makes Thm 8.3 binding.

§9 Personalized TrustRank for consumers

Each consumer supplies a personalized teleport $s_\pi$ representing their own trust priors:

$$t_\pi \;=\; d\big(P+s_\pi u^{\top}\big)t_\pi + (1-d)s_\pi, \qquad \texttt{getTrustVector}(X\mid\pi)\;=\;\tau_{\pi,X}^{(1..5)}$$

The key computational trick: if $s_\pi = \sum_b \omega_b e_b$, then by linearity of personalized PageRank:

$$t_\pi = \sum_b \omega_b\,t_{e_b}$$

Basis vectors $t_{e_b}$ are precomputed once per epoch. Any consumer's personalized view is then a cheap linear combination of cached basis vectors — no new power iteration, just a dot product. A conservative insurance protocol and an aggressive launchpad read the same ledger through different linear combinations.

§10 Algorithm, complexity, on/off-chain split

# EPOCH(t) # ── off-chain (verifiable, ZK-provable execution) ── 1. ingest anchor events Eₜ → build s⁺(k), s⁻(k) (§5) 2. assemble edges (B,q,f) → A, M=rowstoch(A), P=Mᵀ (§2) 3. for k in dims: t(k) ← PowerIter(G⁺(k), s⁺(k)) # TrustRank (TR) δ(k) ← PowerIter(G⁻(k), s⁻(k)) # Anti-TrustRank (ATR) c(k) ← PowerIter(uniform teleport) # spam-mass baseline (§4.3) τ(k) ← gate(t(k), δ(k)); SpamMass ← (c−t)/c 4. weights wᵢ(k) = stakeᵢ·τᵢ(k) 5. Cₜ(k) = Σ wᵢ(k) vᵢ(k) / Σ wᵢ(k) # consensus (§7.2) 6. on any negative anchor: slashᵢⱼ from distrust flux Φ (§6) # ── on-chain (OracleCore-style settlement) ── 7. V(k)_{t+1} = (1-α)V(k)ₜ + α Cₜ(k); publish τ, V, citations 8. ε-N settlement check per dimension; emit timeline event

Complexity. Each power iteration step costs $O(\text{nnz}(A))$. With $d\le0.4$, iterations to reach tolerance $\eta=10^{-6}$:

$$\lceil\log_{1/d}(1/\eta)\rceil = \lceil\log_{2.5}(10^6)\rceil \approx 15 \text{ iterations}$$

15 sequences in parallel, all sharing matrix $P$. For 10,000 entities and 100,000 vouches: well under a second on modern hardware. The low damping that defends us spectrally also makes computation faster — a rare alignment of security and performance incentives.

Open problems

  1. Correlated seed errors. A wrong positive anchor is a trusted-seed poisoning. The formal bound on shift: $\Delta t_\infty \le (1-d)\cdot\Delta s^{+}_\infty$, derivable from the Lipschitz continuity of the Google matrix.
  2. Edge-bond liquidity. A shared-bond pool (similar to a clearinghouse margin system) could reduce honest participation friction without losing the economic deterrent.
  3. Dynamic-graph drift. Incremental PageRank algorithms (Desikan et al. 2005, Bahmani et al. 2010) update only affected entries when a small number of edges change — correctness under TrustRank's changing bonds and freshness decay is open.
  4. Teleport gaming. A consumer concentrating personalized $s_\pi$ on entities they control can engineer a self-serving view. Mitigation: restrict personalized teleports to a vetted canonical basis set.
